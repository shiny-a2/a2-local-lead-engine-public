# Future Email Personalization Evidence Policy

Future emails must feel natural, helpful, and non-advertising. Copy must be based on evidence,
not a generic pitch.

Phase 2 only captures raw evidence. It does not write email copy, generate a subject, or make
final claims.

Evidence may help later phases mention:

- business type
- local area
- likely customer action path
- missing or unclear web presence, only after verification
- category-relevant suggested modules

Avoid manipulative language, fake familiarity, absolute claims, saying "I saw you on Google
Maps", or saying "you have no website" until Phase 4 verification supports it.

Use evidence to create a helpful small suggestion, not a sales blast.
