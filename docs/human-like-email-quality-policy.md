# Human-Like Email Quality Policy

Drafts should sound calm, specific, and useful. They should not sound like agency copy, a mass template, a pitch deck, or a pressure tactic.

Positive signals:
- plain English
- one clear thought per paragraph
- specific business/local/category context
- soft helpful CTA
- restrained offer language

Risk signals:
- hype, urgency, or fear
- competitor comparison
- exaggerated agency language
- generic templates with no local or category anchor
- creepy details such as personal names, raw phone numbers, legal registry details, or opening hours

Phase 8 can approve a draft only for human review. It cannot approve sending.
